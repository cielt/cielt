The Interim Haiti Recovery Commission (IHRC) was established to oversee recovery projects in the wake of the 2010 earthquake. The commission was in charge of ensuring that aid and assistance given to the disaster-stricken nation aligned with the self-determined priorities of the people and government of Haiti. The commission was co-chaired by then prime minister Jean-Max Bellerive and Bill Clinton, former President of the United States.

I was part of the team at President Clinton’s foundation who were charged with redesigning the IHRC web presence. We strove to present important content — mission, recovery goals by sector, and the portfolio of projects registered through the IHRC — in a prominent and well-organized way. We also wanted to suffuse the design with the intrepid, fearless spirit that has characterized the Haitian people through all of history, and made the nation’ motto and rallying cry “L'union fait la force”, part of the site’ theme.

The research group of Dr. Zemer Gitai studies how cellular components are organized and coordinated to carry out vital functions. The lab uses Caulobacter crescentus as a model organism, a widely abundant bacterium that, in reproduction, gives rise to two distinct daughter cells — a sticky-ended, fixative stalked cell and a mobile swarmer cell. The stalked cell can attach to surfaces by a very strong natural glue, while the flagellum-equipped swarmer cell is able to swim, e.g., in search of nutrients. We took inspiration from the unique morphology of this organism, with special attention to its cytoskeleton (the lab’s focus), in coming up with the website design.

While I was working as a technician in the lab of Dr. T. Keith Blackwell, we created a website for his research group at Harvard Medical School (they have since joined the Joslin Diabetes Center). Under Dr. Blackwell’s direction, lab members investigate basic research questions concerning oxidative stress response (linked to, e.g., ageing) and germline development, using the nematode C. Elegans as a model organism. The beautiful simplicity of the worm’s anatomy, along with striking visualizations of protein expression in its tissue, served as interesting themes in my first ever web project.
